The Deteriorating Patient task list is more than an electronic checklist. It is a change in process which aims to improve patient safety by promoting the timely recognition, response and escalation of the deteriorating patient.

The formalisation of this process using IBOX, has allowed us to achieve a more consistent and streamlined response with at risk patients being flagged to team members directly.

The Deteriorating Patient task list is more than an electronic checklist. It is a change in process which aims to improve patient safety by promoting the timely recognition, response and escalation of the deteriorating patient.

The formalisation of this process using IBOX, has allowed us to achieve a more consistent and streamlined response with at risk patients being flagged to team members directly.

It has also provided a mechanism to identify problems in our systems before they pose a risk to patients and a platform for learning and sharing best practice from across the hospital.

Hospital Standardised Mortality Ratio (HSMR)

There has been a sustained reduction in in mortality, this coincides with the introduction of the Deteriorating Patient task list.

Reduction in Harm

Since the introduction of the Deteriorating Patient task list to present there have been zero incidents declared where a failure to recognise and escalate has been identified as a contributory factor.

Preventable Cardiac Arrests

Demonstrates a marked reduction in preventable cardiac arrests

Unexpected Critical Care Admissions

There have been fewer unexpected admissions to critical care from adult inpatient wards
